Trinity Bridge vs BCG, Bain, McKinsey, Slalom
A practical look at where a boutique, AI-operated firm fits alongside the four largest AI consulting practices — and where it doesn't.

For mid-market companies evaluating AI transformation partners, the conventional choices are the major consulting firms — each with a dedicated AI practice. Below is an honest comparison: what each one is built for, where the differences matter, and where Trinity Bridge fits.

The four major AI practices, briefly

BCG X

BCG's tech build and design unit. BCG X ships actual data products and software alongside advisory work, rather than slide-deck strategy alone. Strongest fit for enterprises that want their consultant to also build and operate the systems being recommended.

Bain Vector

Bain's digital and AI practice — Vector spans more than 1,500 data scientists and engineers and has executed thousands of digital projects. Notable recent moves include a 2026 Palantir partnership for AI transformation and a venture ecosystem with seven major VC firms. Strongest fit for clients who want strategy paired with strong vendor and capital relationships.

McKinsey QuantumBlack

QuantumBlack, AI by McKinsey applies hybrid intelligence — combining data science with classic management consulting. Now augmented by the McKinsey-Google Transformation Group (April 2026), pairing QuantumBlack engineers with Google Cloud's forward-deployed engineering teams. Strongest fit for global enterprises with complex multi-business-unit transformations.

Slalom EnhanceIQ

Slalom's AI offering — EnhanceIQ — is an AI-powered accelerator focused on identifying where AI can enhance specific roles and tasks. Slalom was named the 2024 AWS GenAI Consulting Partner of the Year. Strongest fit for organizations already on AWS or Salesforce with concrete workflow-automation goals.

Where Trinity Bridge is different

Trinity Bridge is a boutique firm operated by AI itself — meaning AI agents (alongside human strategists) deliver the engagement work. This shapes three concrete differences:

Dimension Tier-1 firms (BCG, Bain, McKinsey, Slalom) Trinity Bridge
Engagement size Multi-month, multi-team, $250k+ typical floor Discovery to roadmap in weeks; fractional CXO ongoing
Cost structure Senior-partner billing rates + leveraged team AI-operated overhead — published pricing, no leverage pyramid
Best client fit Enterprises with the budget and complexity for full-scale transformation Mid-market firms wanting a small, accountable team focused on operating model change

How to choose

If your organization needs a multi-year, multi-region transformation with deep platform partnerships (Palantir, Google Cloud, AWS), the tier-1 firms are designed for exactly that scale.

If you're a mid-market services firm trying to move from "we've experimented with AI" to "AI runs specific functions of our business" — and you want to work with a small, accountable team rather than a leveraged engagement — Trinity Bridge is built for that profile.

The honest answer in many cases is: pick neither. The right partner depends on whether you need AI strategy at scale (tier-1) or AI operating-model change at mid-market scale (boutique). They are different problems.
